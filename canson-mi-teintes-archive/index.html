Canson Mi Teintes Paper – Professional-Grade Textured Paper for Artists

By Colin · 02 Feb 2025 · 3 min read

Create lasting works with Canson Mi Teintes Paper - acid free, dual-textured, and perfect for pastels, charcoal, and drawing. Shop your favorite colors today.

When it comes to high-quality colored paper, Canson Mi Teintes Paper has long been considered the gold standard. Beloved by students, teachers, and professional artists alike, this paper is available in more than 50 vibrant colors and a variety of formats. It’s known not only for its stunning range but also for its exceptional durability, light resistance, and unique textures that open the door to countless creative possibilities.

Why Canson Mi Teintes Paper Stands Out?

Canson Mi-Teintes isn’t just another colored paper - it’s the result of decades of expertise in papermaking.

Durability You Can Trust

The paper’s colors are added directly into the pulp, ensuring they remain light-resistant and long-lasting. With more than 50% cotton content, ISO 9706 compliance, and a mould-resistant treatment, Mi-Teintes is built to stand the test of time.

Dual-Surface Texture

Each sheet has two usable sides. One features a honeycombed texture, reminiscent of vellum, which provides an ideal grip for dry media. The other is a fine-grain surface, perfect for more delicate effects. The gelatin sizing further reduces pigment absorption, giving artists brighter, more accurate color results.

Versatility Across Mediums

Canson Mi Teintes Paper adapts beautifully to a wide range of techniques. It’s especially suited for:

  • Pastel
  • Charcoal
  • Sanguine
  • Pencil
  • Watercolors
  • Gouache
  • Mixed media and even handicrafts

This makes it one of the most versatile colored papers on the market.

Product Pros & Cons

Before deciding if Canson Mi Teintes Paper is right for you, it helps to weigh its strengths and weaknesses. Here’s what artists generally like - and what they don’t.

Artist Likes

  • Wide product variety with over 50 colors
  • High durability and long-lasting quality
  • Two distinct surface textures in one sheet
  • Works with a broad range of media
  • Thick and sturdy enough for erasing and reworking
  • Strong color blending performance
  • Priced reasonably for the quality

Artist Dislikes

  • Some artists sand the surface for extra smoothness
  • A few report subtle fading of the paper color over time

How to Choose the Right Paper?

With over 50 shades and different formats, it can feel overwhelming to pick the right paper. The good news? Choosing depends mostly on your medium and project.

For Pastels and Charcoal

Darker tones help light colors pop and create strong contrasts. Many pastel artists prefer rich mid-tones like sienna or blue-grey for balanced results.

For Mixed Media

Lighter colors give more flexibility for layering techniques. If you plan on combining pencil, watercolor, and gouache, stick to the fine-grain side for best results.

How to Take care of your Canson Mi Teintes Paper?

Great paper deserves great care. Taking a few extra steps ensures your sheets stay in perfect condition until you’re ready to use them.

Storage Tips

Keep your paper flat and away from direct sunlight to avoid fading over time. A portfolio case or drawer keeps sheets protected from dust and bends.

Handling Tips

Always wash your hands before working, as oils can transfer to the paper. If possible, use clean tissue or interleaving sheets between colors to prevent smudging.

Final Considerations

In the world of colored paper, Canson Mi Teintes Paper continues to hold the crown. Its high cotton content, dual textures, and long-lasting colors make it an outstanding choice for both beginners and professionals. Priced fairly and built for versatility, it’s hard to find a reason not to try it — unless you specifically need an ultra-smooth surface. Otherwise, it’s perfect for pastel, charcoal, pencil work, and educational use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Canson Mi-Teintes archival?

Yes. It’s acid-free, ISO 9706 compliant, and mould-resistant, which makes it suitable for long-lasting artwork.

Q: Can I use watercolor on Canson Mi Teintes Paper?

While it’s primarily designed for pastels and dry media, light watercolor and gouache washes can be applied successfully, especially on the fine-grain side.

Q: Does it handle erasing well?

Yes. Thanks to its thickness and cotton content, you can erase and rework areas without damaging the surface.

Q: How many colors are available?

Mi-Teintes comes in more than 50 different shades, offering one of the widest ranges of colored papers on the market.
