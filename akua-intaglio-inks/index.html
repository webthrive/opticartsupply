The Akua Intaglio Ink is a soy-based type of ink created for printmaking. They contain no dryers, so they allow more time to be brought into the shape that the artist envisions, and they are non-toxic, so they are completely safe to use. Featuring a thick color consistency with minimal amounts of water, these inks will not fade easily unless you wash them with soap.

Versatile Applications of Akua Intaglio Ink

Akua Intaglio Printing Ink is designed with flexibility in mind. Artists can apply them using a brayer for various techniques, including:

  • Relief printing
  • Monotype printing
  • Collagraphs

They're also compatible with a range of surfaces, such as:

  • Plastic plates
  • Woodblocks
  • Linoleum
  • Metal plates

This makes them a valuable option for artists exploring multi-surface or mixed media printmaking.

What Makes Akua Intaglio Inks a Great Choice for Printmaking?

The Intaglio Inks can be applied with a brayer for relief, monotype, and collagraph printmaking techniques. The product allows you to print on plastic, wood, linoleum, or metal plates, and once it’s dry, it’s permanent. Akua offers a range of 25 colors, but more can be derived by using the transparent base, blending medium, and mag mix ink stiffener.

Safe and Non-Toxic for Everyday Use

Akua Intaglio Printing Ink is made from a soy-based formula, so it’s non-toxic and safe to use indoors. They don’t give off strong smells or require harsh chemicals for clean-up. They’re water-based, so great for artists working in classrooms, small studios, or shared spaces. You won’t need solvents or ventilation to use them.

Long Working Time and Easy Cleanup

One of the biggest benefits of Akua Inks is how long they stay wet. You can work slowly and make changes without worrying that the ink will dry too fast. Cleanup is also simple: just use soap and water. The ink stays wet on the plate but dries permanently once printed on paper.

Works on Many Printmaking Surfaces

Akua Intaglio Inks are flexible when it comes to surfaces. You can use them on plastic, metal, wood, or linoleum plates. They roll out smoothly with a brayer and don’t need any added chemicals to perform well. The Akua Intaglio Printmaking Ink's thick consistency also helps produce clear, sharp lines and rich textures on a variety of materials.

Product Pros & Cons of Akua Intaglio Printing Ink

Artist Likes

Artists generally report satisfaction with Akua Intaglio Inks and the various color choices. They seem to be easy to work with, offer good color results, and have unique workability capacity. The fact that these colors are non-toxic and non-flammable makes them suitable for use in classrooms, etc.

  • Easy to work with when wet
  • Stays wet for enough time
  • Non-toxic, non-flammable
  • Thick color consistency
  • Multiple surface/materials coherence
  • Vibrant color

Artist Dislikes

However, the absence of drying agents that are beneficial to some may cause frustration to those living in areas where the humidity of the atmosphere is high. Some report that the color of the Akua Intaglio Printmaking Ink has not dried even after months! In addition to this, when Akua’s Intaglio Inks expire, they instantly become an unusable thin transparent liquid, so pay attention to the expiration date.

  • Completely unusable when it expires
  • Dry times can be a problem when living in humid areas
