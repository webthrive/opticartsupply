Aves Apoxie Sculpt – Epoxy Clay Designed for the Creative Process

By Colin · 24 May 2025 · 3 min read

Create with confidence using Aves Apoxie Sculpt - smooth, detail-friendly epoxy clay that cures strong and takes paint beautifully.

Aves Apoxie Sculpt is a versatile, two-part epoxy clay used by professionals and hobbyists alike. Whether you’re a sculptor, model maker, or DIY enthusiast, this product is designed to deliver high-quality results for your projects. Unlike traditional clays, Apoxie Sculpt combines the benefits of sculpting clay with the adhesive properties of epoxy, making it a go-to material for intricate detailing, durable repairs, and creative artworks.

Why Choose Aves Apoxie Sculpt?

Ease of Use

The Apoxie Sculpt is easy to mix, mold, and apply. It comes in two parts (A and B) that you mix in equal amounts to activate its properties.

Strong Adhesion

It sticks to a wide range of surfaces, including metal, plastic, wood, and glass.

Durability

Once cured, it becomes rock-hard, waterproof, and resistant to heat and chemicals.

Paintable and Sandable

You can sand, carve, drill, and paint it to achieve the desired finish.

Safe and Non-Toxic

The sculpt is safe to use and free from harmful chemicals, making it ideal for both professional and home projects.

How to Use Aves Apoxie Sculpt?

Prepare the Surface

Ensure the surface is clean, dry, and free from grease or dust for better adhesion.

Mix Equal Parts

Combine equal parts of Part A and Part B. Knead thoroughly for at least two minutes until the color is uniform.

Apply and Sculpt

Use tools or your hands to shape and mold the material. Work within the 2–3 hour work time before it begins to set.

Curing Time

Allow 24 hours for the Apoxie Sculpt to fully cure. Once hardened, it can be sanded, painted, or drilled.

Shape, sculpt, and create with confidence - Aves Apoxie Sculpt gives you the strength and detail you need for projects that last.

Popular Uses for Apoxie Sculpt

Artistic Sculptures

Perfect for creating detailed miniatures, sculptures, and ornaments.

Repairs and Restoration

Ideal for repairing broken ceramics, furniture, or household items.

Custom Models

Widely used in model making for hobbies like war gaming, cosplay, and dioramas.

Jewelry Making

Great for crafting custom jewelry pieces with intricate designs.

Tips and Tricks for Best Results

  • Keep your tools and fingers slightly damp to prevent the clay from sticking.
  • Use fine sandpaper for a smooth finish once the material has cured.
  • Store unused portions of Part A and Part B in separate containers to extend shelf life.
  • Experiment with blending colors by mixing in pigments or acrylic paints before curing.

If you’re ready to elevate your craft, give the product a try - it might just become your favorite go-to material.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can Aves Apoxie Sculpt be used outdoors?

Yes, once cured, Apoxie Sculpt is waterproof and can withstand outdoor conditions.

2. How long does the Apoxie Sculpt last after opening?

If stored properly in airtight containers, it can last for several years.

3. Can I speed up the curing process?

While it’s best to let it cure naturally, applying heat with a lamp can slightly speed up the process.

4. Is the Sculpt safe for kids?

Yes, it’s non-toxic, but adult supervision is recommended for younger children.
