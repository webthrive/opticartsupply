How to Choose the Right Safco Flat Files for Your Workflow?

Quick recommendations

  • Opt for welded steel if you frequently open drawers and require long-term rigidity: select the 5-Drawer Steel Flat Files.
  • Choose Facil for dependable, everyday flat storage on a budget, with labelable drawers and stackable growth.
  • Pick Giant Stacking Trays for modular capacity, proofing cycles, and flexible placement near equipment.
  • Add an Art Rack when you also store foam-core, mount boards, panels, or portfolio cases upright.

Space planning tips

  • Use a riser base under stacked 5-drawer units for ergonomic height and toe clearance.
  • Combine one shallow stack for editions with one deeper area (or a tray tower) for folders and backers.
  • Park an Art Rack at the end of your flat-file row to stage boards for framing, mounting, or packaging.

Buying Checklist for Safco Flat Files

Confirm Your Formats

  • Primary sheet sizes (18×24, 22×30, 24×36, 30×42, A-series)
  • Clearance for interleaving (glassine/Mylar) and rigid backers
  • Need for oversize storage or occasional large sheets

Choose Your Build

  • 5-Drawer Steel for premium strength and daily access
  • Facil for cost-effective, stackable flat storage
  • Stacking Trays for modular tiers and WIP staging
  • Art Rack for vertical boards, panels, and cases

Plan the Footprint

  • Floor space and full-extension clearance in front of drawers
  • Desired working height if stacking two 5-drawer units with a base
  • Access lanes for carts, presses, or large-format printers

Labeling & Inventory

  • Consistent drawer labels (size/series/date) and a master map card
  • Colour dots to code projects or media at a glance
  • A simple inventory in Excel, Airtable, or Notion to track location and status

How to Set Up & Care?

Assembly & Placement

Set cabinets on a level floor so drawers track smoothly. If stacking, align the corner guides or manufacturer tabs and secure as instructed. Keep the top clear so dusting is easy and visitors don't treat it as a shelf.

Protecting Surfaces

Interleave charcoal, pastel, and richly inked pieces with glassine. The folder features small works inside rigid sleeves, preventing anything from sliding under larger sheets. Let varnishes and inks cure fully before filing to prevent sticking.

Cleaning & Maintenance

Wipe steel exteriors with a soft, barely damp cloth and dry immediately. Gently dust wood finishes; avoid using abrasives. Vacuum beneath and behind units to limit dust migration. If a drawer drags, clear the slide rather than forcing it to move.
