Looking for a vibrant, versatile art tool that bridges the gap between drawing and painting? Derwent Inktense Blocks are the perfect choice! These watersoluble ink blocks offer rich, intense colors that bring your artwork to life—whether you’re sketching, painting, or experimenting with mixed media.

Let’s explore how Inktense Blocks can transform your creative process and help you make bold, lasting art.

Why Choose Derwent Inktense Blocks?

1. Dual-Purpose Magic: Dry or Wet Use

Use these Inktense Blocks dry for bold, vivid lines or soft shading. Add water and they transform into vibrant, ink-like paint with rich, translucent washes. Once dry, the color is permanent, allowing you to layer without smudging.

2. Versatile and Easy to Use

The chunky block shape makes it easy to create fine lines, bold strokes, or broad washes. Use them directly on dry or wet paper, dip the block in water, or even shave off pigment to mix custom shades.

3. Ideal for Mixed Media Projects

Perfect for more than just paper! Inktense Blocks work beautifully on fabric, making them great for silk paintings, quilt designs, or adding color to tote bags and apparel. Plus, they pair perfectly with Derwent Inktense Pencils for added detail.

4. Permanent, Layerable Color

Unlike watercolor, Inktense dries permanently, so you can layer colors without blending or lifting previous layers. This makes them ideal for building depth and vibrant layers in your artwork.

5. Brilliant, Vibrant Colors

From deep blues to bright reds, Inktense Blocks deliver pure, vibrant hues. The colors stay bold and eye-catching, even after drying.

Creative Ways to Use Derwent Inktense Blocks

1. Bold Landscapes and Abstract Art

Use dry blocks for expressive sketching and add water for smooth, vibrant washes. Perfect for loose, colorful scenes and bold designs.

2. Textile and Fabric Art

Activate the pigment with water to create lasting designs on fabric—great for silk paintings, quilts, and custom clothing.

3. Mixed Media Masterpieces

Combine with watercolors, colored pencils, or acrylics for rich textures and layers. The permanent finish allows for seamless integration.

4. Stamping and Stenciling

Shave pigment off the block to mix colors or apply directly to stamps for bright, custom prints.

How to Use Derwent Inktense Blocks?

Step 1: Choose Your Surface

  • Works on paper, fabric, or wood.
  • For fabric, wet the material slightly before applying.

Step 2: Apply Dry or Wet

  • Dry: Draw directly for bold strokes and sketching.
  • Wet: Add water for soft, translucent ink-like washes.

Step 3: Layer and Blend

  • Once dry, colors are permanent, allowing for layering without blending.
  • Mix colors directly on the paper or blend on a palette.

Step 4: Finish and Set

  • Let your work dry naturally.
  • On fabric, heat set with an iron to make designs washable.

Tips for Best Results

  • Start Light: Begin with light layers and build up color gradually.
  • Use a Spray Bottle: Mist your surface lightly for smooth blending.
  • Layer Colors: Once dry, layer more color for depth and dimension.
  • Experiment on Fabric: Try silk, cotton, or canvas for unique effects.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are Inktense Blocks the same as watercolor paints?

A: No. Inktense Blocks dry permanently, unlike watercolors, which can be reactivated with water.

Q: Can I use them on fabric?

A: Yes! Apply to wet fabric for vibrant designs, and heat-set with an iron to make it permanent.

Q: Do they blend well with other mediums?

A: Absolutely! They work great with watercolors, pencils, and acrylics.
