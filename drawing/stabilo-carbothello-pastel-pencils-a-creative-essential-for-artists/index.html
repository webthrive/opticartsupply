Stabilo CarbOthello Pastel Pencils: A Creative Essential for Artists

Looking for a tool that gives you the vibrant, soft feel of pastels with the precision of a pencil? Meet the Stabilo CarbOthello Pastel Pencils - a favorite among artists for their rich pigments and smooth blending. Whether you're sketching, shading, or blending, these pencils make it easy to create bold and beautiful artwork.

Why Choose Stabilo CarbOthello Pastel Pencils?

Stabilo Pastel Pencils offer bold and vivid color, which blends easily. Besides, you can add water to have more precise and versatile designs.

1. Bold, Vivid Colors

Each pencil is packed with high-quality, lightfast pigments that stay bright over time. Your artwork will remain as vibrant as the day you created it.

2. Smooth Blending

The soft, creamy lead makes blending effortless. You can layer colors, soften edges, and create seamless transitions.

3. Water-Soluble for Extra Effects

Want to try something different? Add water with a brush to create soft watercolor effects. You can also apply the pencils to damp paper for even richer colors.

4. Precise and Versatile

The large-diameter lead allows for both broad strokes and fine details. Use it like a pastel stick or sharpen it to a fine point for intricate designs.

5. Easy to Erase

Made a mistake? No problem. Stabilo CarbOthello Pastel Pencils erase easily, giving you more control over your work.

Ready to add depth and vibrancy to your art? Try Stabilo Pastel Pencils and see your creativity come to life!

How to Use the Pastel Pencils?

Step 1: Start with a Good Surface

Use textured paper or pastel paper for the best grip and blending.

Step 2: Layer and Blend

  • Apply colors lightly and build up layers for depth.
  • Use a blending stump, finger, or brush for smooth transitions.

Step 3: Add Water for Watercolor Effects

  • Apply a wet brush over the pigment to create a soft, blended effect.
  • For a stronger color, draw directly on damp paper.

Step 4: Sharpen for Details

Use the included sharpener (in larger sets) to keep the tip fine for small details or lettering.

Step 5: Protect Your Work

Once your piece is complete, spray it with a pastel fixative to prevent smudging.
