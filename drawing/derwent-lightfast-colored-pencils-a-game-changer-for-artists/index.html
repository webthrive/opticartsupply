Derwent Lightfast Colored Pencils: A Game-Changer for Artists

By Colin · 21 May 2025 · 3 min read

Create lasting art with Derwent Lightfast Pencils - oil-based, richly pigmented, and 100% lightfast for true professional results.

If you’re an artist looking for high-quality, long-lasting colored pencils, Derwent Lightfast Colored Pencils might be the perfect choice. These professional-grade pencils offer rich, vibrant colors that are designed to resist fading for up to 100 years under museum conditions. Whether you’re a seasoned artist or just starting out, these pencils will help bring your artwork to life.

Why Choose Derwent Lightfast Colored Pencils?

1. 100% Lightfast Guarantee

One of the biggest concerns for artists is color fading over time. Many traditional colored pencils lose their vibrancy when exposed to light. However, Derwent Lightfast Pencils are made with pigments that resist UV light, ensuring that your artwork remains as vivid as the day you created it. These pencils meet the ASTM D6901 international lightfast standard, making them a reliable choice for professional and archival work.

2. Smooth, Oil-Based Formula

Unlike wax-based colored pencils, which can leave a waxy buildup or "bloom," Derwent Lightfast Colored Pencils use an oil-based core. This unique composition allows for:

  • Smooth, effortless blending
  • Rich, opaque layers
  • A buttery texture similar to oil pastels
  • Less breakage and more durability

3. Versatile and Easy to Use

Derwent Lightfast pencils work well with various techniques and surfaces. They are ideal for:

  • Layering and blending – Create soft transitions and smooth shading
  • Mixed media projects – Use them with markers, inks, and watercolors
  • Burnishing – Achieve a polished, glossy finish by layering and blending
  • Solvent blending – Use mineral spirits or blending mediums for a painterly effect

4. Wide Range of Colors

With up to 100 colors available, Derwent Lightfast offers an impressive range of shades, from deep earth tones to bright, bold hues. The high pigment concentration ensures vivid and true-to-life colors that work well on various surfaces, including paper, canvas, and wood.

So why not give them a try? Pick up a set of Derwent Colored Pencils today and take your artwork to the next level!

How to Get the Best Results with Derwent Lightfast Pencils?

1. Experiment with Blending Techniques

Because these pencils have an oil-based core, they blend beautifully. Try using a blending stump, tissue, or even your fingers to create smooth transitions between colors.

2. Layering for Depth and Richness

To achieve depth in your artwork, apply multiple layers. Start with light pressure and gradually build up the intensity. You can also mix colors directly on the paper by layering different shades.

3. Use a Solvent for a Painterly Effect

Want to mimic the look of oil painting? Try using a solvent like odorless mineral spirits to dissolve the pigments and create a smooth, painted effect. This technique works great for realistic portraits and landscapes.

4. Keep Your Pencils Sharp

A sharp point is essential for adding fine details and crisp edges. Use a high-quality sharpener designed for colored pencils to maintain a fine tip.
