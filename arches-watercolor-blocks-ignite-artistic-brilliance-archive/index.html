Frequently Asked Questions on Arches Watercolor Blocks

Q: What makes the blocks different from regular watercolor paper pads?

A: Each block is glued on all four sides, so the paper stays perfectly flat while you paint. This prevents warping and buckling, even with heavy washes.

Q: How do I remove a sheet from the block?

A: Use a palette knife or letter opener to gently slide under the unglued corner, then work your way around the edges until the sheet is free.

Q: Are Arches Watercolor Blocks 100% cotton?

A: Yes. They’re made from 100% cotton fibers, which gives the paper exceptional strength, durability, and excellent water absorption.

Q: Are they suitable for other media besides watercolor?

A: Absolutely. Many artists use them for gouache, ink, and even light acrylic work, as long as the paint isn’t applied too thickly.

Q: Are Arches Watercolor Blocks acid-free and archival?

A: Yes. The paper is acid-free, pH-neutral, and archival, so your paintings will remain vibrant for years without yellowing.
