Masterpiece Artfix Acrylic Primed Linen Canvas: Built to Last, Made to Paint

By Colin · 21 May 2025 · 3 min read

Shop Masterpiece Artfix Acrylic Primed Linen Canvas – professional-grade, pre-primed linen for acrylic painting. Strong, smooth, and ready to use right out of the box.

If you're looking for a high-quality canvas that can handle serious acrylic work, the Masterpiece Artfix Acrylic Primed Linen Canvas is one you’ll want to know about. It’s not your average pre-stretched canvas—this one combines old-school craftsmanship and modern performance to give artists a solid surface with a long-lasting structure.

Let’s examine what makes this canvas different and why it might be the upgrade your studio has been waiting for.

What's So Special About This Canvas?

The Masterpiece Artfix Acrylic Primed Linen Canvas stands out for its professional-quality surface, tight drum like tension, and archival-grade linen, perfect for artists who demand durability and precision.

1. Premium Belgian Linen

It starts with the linen. Masterpiece uses archival-quality Belgian linen, which is known for its strength and smooth texture. Linen has been trusted by artists for centuries—it doesn’t stretch out as easily as cotton and holds up better over time. If you plan to create something you want to last, linen is the way to go.

2. Hand-Primed for a Professional Finish

Each canvas is primed by hand with two coats of size and two coats of an acrylic-based primer. That process gives you a beautifully even surface that’s ready for acrylics. With a primed weight of 12 oz, it feels sturdy and substantial under your brush, which is precisely what you want when laying down layers of paint.

3. Built for Flexibility and Strength

The edges are staple-free, so this canvas is gallery-ready if you’re into that modern, frameless look. Plus, the folded corners and uncut selvage mean you can re-stretch it if you ever need to—something serious painters and conservators will appreciate.

Smart Design That Stays Strong

Clever Frame Construction

This canvas isn’t just about surface quality. The frame matters, too—and Masterpiece gets it right. It features a tongue-and-groove bracing system that lets the stretcher bars expand as needed without glue or staples. That’s a big deal because it helps the canvas stay tight over time.

Cross-Bracing for Larger Sizes

If you’re working on a larger piece, you're covered. Sizes 16" x 20" and up come with cross-bracing to keep the frame extra strong and the canvas tension nice and even. There will be no sagging or warping.

Two Profiles to Choose From: Masterpiece Artfix Acrylic Primed Linen Canvas

Depending on how you want your work to be displayed, you can choose between two canvas depths:

Vincent Pro

  • 7/8" deep
  • 5/16" relief between the bar face and the canvas back
  • Great for traditional framing or a slimmer profile

Monet Pro

  • 1-1/2" deep
  • 1/2" relief
  • A bolder, modern look that’s perfect for frameless or gallery-style presentations

Both profiles are built with the same quality materials and attention to detail—you just have to pick the one that fits your style.

What Is This Canvas Good For?

  • Acrylic painting with lots of layering or texture
  • Mixed media that includes heavy-bodied paints or gel mediums
  • Professional work intended for galleries or collectors
  • Projects where longevity matters and you want your canvas to hold up over time

Frequently Asked Questions on Masterpiece Artfix Acrylic Primed Linen Canvas

What makes the Masterpiece Artfix Linen Canvas good for painting?

This canvas is made from strong linen and has an even, acrylic-primed surface. It holds paint well and doesn’t easily bend or sag over time.

Can I use oil or acrylic paints on this canvas?

Yes, this canvas works great with both oil and acrylic paints. The acrylic primer keeps paint from soaking through and helps colors stay bright.

Is the canvas already stretched and ready to use?

Yes, the canvas comes pre-stretched on a strong wooden frame. You can start painting right away—no need to prep or stretch it yourself.
