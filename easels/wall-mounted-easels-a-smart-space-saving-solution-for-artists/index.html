Wall Mounted Easels: A Smart Space-Saving Solution for Artists

By Colin · 23 Jul 2025 · 3 min read

Shop wall mounted easels for compact, sturdy canvas support. Save space without sacrificing stability; ideal for studios, schools, and home artists.

Every artist needs a workspace that suits their medium and habits. But when floor space is limited, traditional studio easels can take up more room than they should. That’s where Wall Mounted Easels come in. These easels are designed to mount directly onto your wall, offering the stability of an H-frame with none of the bulk. Whether you're working in a compact studio, a shared space, or simply want a cleaner layout, a wall mounted art easel is a practical alternative.

Why Wall Mounted Easels Are Worth Considering?

Wall mounted easels give artists the freedom to work on large canvases without sacrificing studio space. Mounted securely to a wall, they eliminate the need for floor support while providing the same functionality you'd expect from traditional wall mount easels.

Here’s what makes a wall mounted art easel a strong choice:

  • Space Efficiency: Ideal for studios with limited room
  • Stable Support: Easily holds large and heavy canvases
  • Adjustability: Many models allow vertical and forward tilt
  • Comfort: Height can often be adjusted for seated or standing work

The Best Wall Mounted Easel, for example, is built for serious work in tight spaces. It can support canvases up to 88" in height and adjusts smoothly with a crank system. The forward tilt is especially useful for pastel artists to reduce glare during painting sessions.

FAQs about Wall Mounted Easels

Can it support large canvases?

Yes. Models like the Best Wallmount Easel can hold canvases up to 88" tall.

Do they come pre-assembled?

Some assembly is usually required, but most brands provide clear instructions and mounting hardware.

Are these easels adjustable?

Most models include vertical movement, and some allow forward tilt, mainly useful for pastel or glare reduction.

What mediums work best with these easels?

They’re great for oil, acrylic, casein, and pastel, just make sure the tilt is adjustable for the medium you use.
