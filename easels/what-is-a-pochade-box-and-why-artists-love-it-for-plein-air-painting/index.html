What Is a Pochade Box and Why Artists Love It for Plein Air Painting?

By Colin · 27 Jul 2025 · 4 min read

Take your art anywhere with a pochade box - compact, portable, and built for plein air painting. Perfect for oils, acrylics, and travel sketching.

Working outdoors requires an efficient setup, and a pochade box makes that possible. Designed for portability, these compact boxes let artists carry their paints, brushes, and surface all in one unit. Whether you’re sketching landscapes in the park or working on small studies in your backyard, these boxes help you stay focused and organized. This guide breaks down everything you need to know about these boxes, including the top models to consider and where to purchase them.

What Makes a Pochade Box Useful?

A plein air pochade box combines convenience and function. It’s a small, self-contained unit that serves as your easel, palette, and storage case all in one. Many boxes are designed to rest on your lap, attach to a tripod, or be placed on a table. 

Key Features:

  • Compact Size: Most fit easily in a backpack or tote.
  • Integrated Palette: Usually built right into the box.
  • Canvas Support: Holds small panels (typically up to 15–17 inches).
  • Storage Compartments: Keeps brushes, paints, and accessories organized.
  • Versatility: Works for oil, acrylic, and even watercolor or pastel (with adaptations).

A pochade box easel is an efficient solution for both beginners and experienced artists who value mobility without giving up essential features.

Types of Pochade Boxes for Different Painting Styles

When shopping for a plein air pochade box, it helps to understand the available options. While the concept remains the same, slight variations in design can better suit your specific medium or working style.

Oil and Acrylic Painting Boxes

This box typically comes with a removable glass or wood palette and has enough depth to hold tubes, brushes, and medium jars. They’re great for use with easels or tripods.

Watercolor and Pastel Adaptations

While plein air pochade boxes are usually designed for oils or acrylics, many brands offer add-ons or versions that support watercolor pans or pastel trays. Such box easels might come with different palette surfaces or more shallow compartments.

Tripod-Compatible Boxes

Some boxes, like the Sienna or Guerrilla Painter models, are built to mount on standard camera tripods, giving you height and angle flexibility while painting outdoors.

Final Thoughts

A pochade box can become one of your most valued art tools, especially if you enjoy painting on location or need a space-saving solution. It keeps your setup tidy and helps you focus more on painting than packing. From beginners to advanced artists, everyone benefits from the functionality and freedom this type of box offers.

FAQs About Pochade Boxes

What is the ideal canvas size for this box?

Most boxes are built to hold panels up to 15" or 17", depending on the model.

Can I use it for watercolor or pastels?

Yes, with slight modifications or add-ons. Brands like Sienna offer palettes designed specifically for watercolor and pastel use.

How do I mount this box to a tripod?

These come with a standard 1/4"-20 thread that fits most camera tripods.

Do all of these come with a palette?

Most do, but some may offer removable palettes or require you to purchase a palette separately.
