Make Learning Interactive: The Complete Guide to Classroom Easels

By Colin · 30 Jul 2025 · 3 min read

Upgrade your classroom with durable, easy-to-use easels designed for interactive learning. Shop top-rated styles for teachers, students, and creative lessons.

A classroom easel is more than a piece of classroom furniture. It’s a tool that helps teachers connect better with students. Whether it’s used for drawing, writing, or displaying work, a good easel makes learning more visible, organized, and interactive.

You’ll often find easels for classrooms​ in early learning environments, art rooms, and elementary schools. They help teachers write down lesson goals, show examples, or even demonstrate techniques in front of the class. Many classroom whiteboard easels come with built-in whiteboards, storage trays, or rolling wheels to suit different classroom needs.

Types of Classroom Easels and Their Uses

When shopping for an easel for classroom use, it helps to know the different types and what they’re best for.

1. Teaching Easels

These are built for demonstrations. Teachers can clip on chart paper, draw diagrams, or write notes. Many models include shelves and marker trays.

Best for: Daily instruction, note-taking, and demonstrations.

2. Classroom Whiteboard Easels

Equipped with dry-erase surfaces, these are perfect for lessons that need to be changed frequently. Some models have a magnetic surface to attach visuals.

Best for: Solving math problems, writing key points, or playing interactive games.

3. Chart Stand Easels

These are more mobile and can hold large paper pads or magnetic boards. Some have hooks or storage bins to keep materials close.

Best for: Group work, anchor charts, and mobile teaching.

4. Multi-Purpose Easels

Some classroom whiteboard easels offer both dry-erase surfaces and flipchart holders. They give you flexibility in how you teach each day.

Best for: Teachers who want one easel that does it all.Want to make your classroom lessons more engaging and interactive? Start with the right tools.

Frequently Asked Questions on Classroom Easels?

Look for durability, mobility (if needed), dry-erase or paper surfaces, and bonus features like storage or magnetic boards.

Can I use the same easel in different classrooms?

Yes. Many models have wheels or foldable designs to move easily between rooms.

Are classroom easels only for younger students?

Not at all. Some are designed for art rooms or higher grade levels with more durable construction.

Is a chart stand different from a teaching easel?

Yes. Chart stands are often mobile and designed to hold flip charts or pads. Teaching easels may have more features like trays, storage, and dry-erase boards.
