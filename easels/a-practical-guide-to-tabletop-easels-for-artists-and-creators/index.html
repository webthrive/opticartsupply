A Practical Guide to Tabletop Easels for Artists and Creators

By Colin · 23 Jul 2025 · 5 min read

Buy the best tabletop easels for painting, drawing, and art display. Compact, foldable, and ideal for small spaces, students, and travel use.

If you’re working in a compact space, creating smaller artworks, or just need something more flexible than a floor easel, a tabletop easel can be exactly what you need. Whether you're painting at your kitchen table, showcasing finished pieces at an art fair, or sketching while traveling, these easels offer a compact and functional solution. This guide explores how to choose the right one, the types available, and where to find the best brands.

Why Choose a Tabletop Easel?

A tabletop display easel is a small, adjustable stand designed to hold your artwork while you create or to display your finished work. These easels rest on desks or tables and are ideal for artists who work while seated or have limited floor space. Because they’re compact, lightweight, and often foldable, they’re easy to move around and perfect for both studio and portable use.

Unlike full-size studio easels, tabletop versions are easier to store and transport. Whether you paint with oils, acrylics, or watercolors, a tabletop easel for painting tilts to various angles so you can work comfortably and with more control. Whereas some easels even include drawers or compartments for your tools.

Types of Tabletop Easels and Their Uses

The tabletop easel for painting includes several types, each serving a specific purpose depending on your medium and workflow.

Sketchbox Easels

These tabletop easels are a favorite among mobile artists. They fold into a briefcase-like shape and often include compartments for brushes, paints, and pencils.

Best for:

  • Traveling or plein air painting
  • Artists who prefer integrated storage
  • Quick setup and breakdown

Highlights:

  • Built-in drawers or palettes
  • Folds flat for easy carrying
  • Adjustable angle and height
  • Great for all media types

Display Easels

Unlike working easels, these are designed purely for presentation. A tabletop display easel will hold a finished canvas, print, or sign upright on a table or shelf.

Best for:

  • Art exhibitions or open studios
  • Presenting signs, menus, or framed photos
  • Retail or gallery displays

Highlights:

  • Decorative design
  • Holds lightweight items
  • Easy to set up and move
  • Not meant for active painting

Desk or H-Frame Easels

These tabletop H-frame easels are stable, miniaturized versions of traditional H-frame studio easels. Designed for heavier canvases, they offer better support during longer painting sessions.

Best for:

  • Home studios
  • Artists who want more stability
  • Working on medium-sized canvases

Highlights:

  • Heavy base, stable structure
  • Adjustable tilt and canvas height
  • Typically wood construction
  • Can support both painting and drawing

A-Frame Table Easels

Lightweight and easier to store, A-frame easels are good for smaller workspaces. They usually offer a simple three-legged design.

Best for:

  • Beginners
  • Hobby artists
  • Occasional painting sessions

Highlights:

  • Folds flat
  • Lightweight
  • Affordable
  • Takes up minimal space

FAQs About Tabletop Easels

Can it be used for oil painting?

Yes. Many models are sturdy enough to support small to medium canvases for oil painting, especially H-frame or sketchbox designs.

Are these easels adjustable?

Most are. You can tilt them to different angles to suit your painting style or medium.

What’s the difference between a tabletop easel and a tabletop display easel?

Tabletop easels for painting are for showcasing finished art, not for working on it. Whereas tabletop easels are built for active use during painting or drawing.

Do these easels come with storage?

Some models, especially sketchbox easels, include built-in drawers for brushes, paints, and tools.
