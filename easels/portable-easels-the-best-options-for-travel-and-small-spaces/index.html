Portable Easels: The Best Options for Travel and Small Spaces

By Colin · 27 Jul 2025 · 5 min read

Looking for a portable easel that’s strong, lightweight and foldable? Explore the best options for artists who paint on the go or in tight spaces.

Creating art outdoors or in compact spaces requires tools that match the pace and setting. That’s where a portable easel comes in. Designed for mobility and versatility, these easels offer artists the freedom to paint anywhere, from sunlit parks to small home studios. In this article, you’ll find everything you need to know about choosing the right easel for portability, the best models to consider, and where to buy them.

Why Choose a Portable Easel?

The biggest benefit of this easel is flexibility. Unlike studio easels, which stay fixed in one place, portable ones fold down, weigh less, and are easy to carry. Whether you're working on oil, watercolor, acrylic, or pastel, these easels can handle a range of techniques and media.

A well-made portable art easel supports your work while keeping supplies close. Many even come with built-in storage, adjustable angles, and extendable legs to suit both seated and standing positions.

Key Features to Look For:

  • Adjustability: Allows you to work at different angles and heights.
  • Storage Options: French portable easel for painting and sketchbox styles have space for brushes, paint, and more.
  • Weight: Lighter easels are easier to carry, but heavier ones offer better stability.
  • Canvas Size Capacity: Some easels can handle large canvases up to 72".
  • Material: Wood and aluminum are common; wood offers a classic feel, while metal is lighter.

Types of Portable Art Easel

When shopping for a portable art easel, it's useful to know the various types available. Each one is designed with a specific kind of artist or use in mind.

French Style Easels

French easels offer a mix of portable easel stand and storage box. When fully opened, they resemble a studio easel. When packed, they become compact boxes with handles. Artists love them for outdoor work thanks to their built-in drawer and tripod legs.

Sketchbox Easels

These easels are similar to French easels but often simpler in design. They include storage compartments and can double as tabletop easels. Great for artists with limited space.

Pochade Boxes

Small and compact, this portable easel for painting is perfect for plein air painting. The lid acts as the easel surface, making it ideal for working on small canvases or panels.

Folding Tripod Easels

Lightweight and easy to carry, these easels are often made of aluminum or wood. Many tripod models fold down into compact shapes, which are perfect for quick setups.

FAQs About Portable eEasel for Painting

What is the difference between a sketchbox and a French easel?

Both offer storage and portability, but French easels include tripod legs and more adjustability, making them better for plein air painting.

Can I use this easel for large canvases?

Yes. Some models can hold canvases up to 72", though you’ll need to check product specs before buying.

Are metal or wooden easels better?

Wooden easels offer stability and a traditional feel, while metal easels are lighter and often easier to carry.

Do all portable easel stands come with storage?

No. Sketchbox and French easels usually do. Tripod and pochade boxes vary, so check before buying.
