Wooden Easels for Artists: A Reliable Choice for Every Studio

By Colin · 27 Jul 2025 · 4 min read

Shop high-quality wooden easels for artists of all levels. Find stable, long-lasting options perfect for studios, classrooms, or home art setups

Every artist, whether just beginning or deeply experienced, benefits from having the right tools. And when it comes to painting or displaying artwork, a wooden easel can be one of the most important tools in your space. These easels offer more than just support; they create a comfortable workspace and bring a traditional look that fits into any studio or room.

In this article, we’ll walk you through what to look for in a wood easel, share trusted options from top brands, and help you decide which one suits your creative needs best.

Why Wooden Easels Are a Go-To Choice

If you’re wondering why artists often prefer these easels over other types, it’s mostly because of their strength, natural feel, and versatility.

What Makes Them Stand Out?

Wooden art easels are typically made from materials like beechwood or pine, which offer the right mix of durability and lightweight handling. They're also easy on the eyes, fitting well in both personal and professional settings.

Here’s what else makes them a favorite:

  • Adjustable design: Most models let you tweak the height and angle, which helps you work more comfortably.
  • Sturdy base: Even when you're working on larger canvases, a well-made wooden easel stand stays firm.
  • Multiple uses: From painting and sketching to simply showcasing your finished work, these easels do it all.
  • Types to match your space:
    • Floor easels are ideal for studios.
    • Tabletop easels work great in tighter spaces.
    • Display easels are more for presenting artwork, not creating it.

How to Pick the Right Wooden Easel Stand?

There’s no one-size-fits-all solution when it comes to easels. Your choice depends on how you work, where you work, and what you're painting.

Ask Yourself:

1. How much space do you have?

  • Small space? Go for a foldable tabletop or A-frame wooden art easel.
  • Got a studio? A larger H-frame might suit you better.

2. What size canvases do you usually use?

  • If you work large, make sure your easel can handle the height and weight.
  • Smaller pieces? Tabletop models will do just fine.

3. What medium do you use?

  • Oils and acrylics need upright support.
  • Watercolors and pastels work better on easels that can tilt flat.

4. Do you move around a lot?

  • If yes, look for something portable. French and pochade easels are great options.

Final Thoughts

A wooden easel stand isn’t just a tool, but a part of your creative rhythm. It helps you stay organized, supports your canvas at the right angle, and adds a professional touch to your space. Frequently Asked Questions on Wooden Easels

What type of wood easel is best for beginners?

A-frame or tabletop easels are great starting points. They’re easy to use and store.

Are these easels good for watercolors?

Yes, especially if the easel can tilt flat. That helps control how the paint moves.

How do I take care of my easel?

Keep it clean by wiping it regularly. If it gets painted on it, use a damp cloth or fine sandpaper.

Can I use a wooden art easel for digital drawing tablets?

Some artists do. If it holds the device securely and lets you adjust the angle, it can work well.
