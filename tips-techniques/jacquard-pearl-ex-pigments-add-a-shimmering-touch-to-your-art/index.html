Jacquard Pearl-Ex Pigments: Add a Shimmering Touch to Your Art

By Colin · 22 May 2025 · 3 min read

If you're looking to add a stunning, shimmering effect to your artwork, Jacquard Pearl-Ex Pigments are exactly what you need. Whether you’re a painter, sculptor, calligrapher, or jewelry maker, these versatile pigments can take your creations to the next level.

Let’s dive into what makes Pearl-Ex pigments so special, how to use them, and why they’re a must-have for any artist.

What Are Jacquard Pearl-Ex Pigments?

Pearl-Ex pigments are ultra-fine, powdered mica pigments that give a beautiful pearlescent or metallic sheen to artwork. Unlike regular metallic paints, they won’t fade, tarnish, or rust over time, making them perfect for long-lasting projects.

These pigments are non-toxic and can be used in a wide range of artistic applications, from painting and sculpture to stamping, resin work, and even pottery. They are also heat-resistant, meaning they can be fired in a kiln up to 600°F (316°C) without losing their brilliance.

Why Choose Pearl-Ex Pigments?

There are plenty of reasons why artists love Pearl-Ex pigments. Here are some of the biggest advantages:

1. Extreme Versatility

Pearl-Ex pigments can be mixed into almost anything, including:

  • Acrylic, oil, and watercolor paints
  • Resin and epoxy
  • Wax for candle making
  • Varnishes and glazes
  • Polymer clay and paper pulp
  • Calligraphy inks and printing mediums

Whether you want to create glowing paintings, shimmering jewelry, or metallic calligraphy, these pigments have you covered.

2. Long-Lasting and Fade-Resistant

Unlike some metallic paints that lose their shine over time, Pearl-Ex pigments are known for their color stability. They won’t rust, tarnish, or fade, making them ideal for archival and decorative projects.

3. Easy to Use

There’s no complicated process to follow—just mix the pigment into your chosen medium and apply it however you like. You can even use them dry for a soft shimmer, then seal with a fixative for a lasting effect.

4. Safe and Non-Toxic

Jacquard Pearl-Ex pigments are completely non-toxic, so they’re safe for artists of all ages. They’re also odor-free, making them a great choice for indoor use.

How to Use Pearl-Ex Pigments

These pigments are incredibly easy to use, and there are multiple ways to apply them depending on the effect you’re going for. Here are a few ideas:

1. Mixing with Paints

Simply blend the pigment into acrylic, oil, or watercolor paint to add a pearlescent finish. Start with a small amount and build up for more intensity.

2. Creating Custom Inks

For calligraphy or stamping, mix the pigment with gum arabic and water to create rich, metallic inks.

3. Adding to Resin or Clay

Mix the pigment directly into resin or polymer clay for a shimmering effect. You can also dust it onto clay before baking for a soft, glowing finish.

4. Using Dry for Highlights

Brush the pigment directly onto paper, fabric, or other surfaces for a subtle shimmer, then seal it with a fixative spray to keep it in place.

FAQs

Can Pearl-Ex pigments be used on fabric?

Yes, but they need to be mixed with a fabric medium or set with heat to stay permanent.

Do they work with airbrushes?

Yes, when mixed with a suitable binder like acrylic medium or gum arabic.

Are these pigments waterproof?

Not on their own, but you can mix them with waterproof mediums or seal them with a clear coat.
