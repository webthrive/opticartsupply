Artist’s Color Wheels

By Colin · 22 May 2025 · 3 min read

Whether you’re just starting your art journey or you’re a seasoned creator, the Artist’s Color Wheel is an essential tool. It helps you understand how colors mix, harmonize, and interact, making it easier to create beautiful, balanced artwork.

Let’s explore what makes this color wheel special, how it can improve your skills, and which version is right for you.

What Is an Artist’s Color Wheel?

The Artist’s Color Wheel is a visual guide that shows the relationships between colors. With a circular design, it illustrates:

  • Color Mixing: How primary, secondary, and tertiary colors interact.
  • Color Harmonies: Complementary, analogous, and triadic combinations for creating balanced compositions.
  • Gray Scale: Helpful for understanding value and contrast.

Printed on sturdy paperboard, this tool is built to last and is easy to use in studios, classrooms, or on the go.

Why Choose the Artist’s Color Wheel?

1. Learn Color Mixing

The color wheel shows how colors blend to form new shades. It’s an excellent reference for mixing paints and understanding how hues work together.

2. Discover Color Harmonies

Not sure which colors look good together? The wheel illustrates harmonious combinations, helping you choose palettes that are visually pleasing.

3. Easy to Use

Whether you’re working on a painting or planning a design, the color wheel is a quick, convenient guide.

4. Sturdy and Long-Lasting

Made from heavy-duty paperboard, the wheel is durable enough for regular use and can withstand classroom or studio environments.

5. Great for All Ages

From beginners learning the basics to professionals refining their palettes, the Artist’s Color Wheel is perfect for anyone interested in color.

Different Versions of the Artist’s Color Wheel

Standard Wheel (9-1/4" Diameter)

The classic size, ideal for studio or personal use.
Why Choose It?
Perfect for most artists. Easy to handle and read while working on projects.

Pocket Wheel (5" Diameter)

A compact version of the standard wheel that fits easily in a bag or sketchbook.
Why Choose It?
Great for artists on the go or for quick reference during outdoor sketching sessions.

Big Classroom Wheel (26" Diameter)

Designed for classroom use, this larger wheel makes it easier to teach color theory to groups.
Why Choose It?
Ideal for teachers or group workshops where everyone needs a clear view.

How to Use the Artist’s Color Wheel

Using the color wheel is simple. Here’s how to get started:

1. Explore Color Mixing

Look at how primary colors (red, blue, yellow) mix to form secondary colors (green, orange, purple) and beyond.

2. Experiment with Harmonies

Use the wheel to find complementary, analogous, or triadic colors for your palette. This ensures balance in your artwork.

3. Check the Gray Scale

Reference the gray scale on the wheel to understand light and dark values, helping you create depth and contrast.

4. Keep It Handy

Place the wheel near your workspace for easy access whenever you’re choosing colors or planning a composition.

Why Every Artist Needs One

The Artist’s Color Wheel is more than just a reference—it’s a learning tool that builds your understanding of color relationships. Here’s why artists love it:

  • It Saves Time: Quickly decide on colors without trial and error.
  • It Boosts Confidence: Know your colors will work well together before you start.
  • It Inspires Creativity: Experiment with combinations you might not have considered.

Final Thoughts

The Artist’s Color Wheel is a simple yet powerful tool that every artist should have. It helps you understand color mixing, find harmonious palettes, and create more balanced, eye-catching artwork.

Whether you’re painting, designing, or teaching, this versatile tool makes color theory accessible and fun.
Ready to explore the world of color? Pick up an Artist’s Color Wheel today and watch your creativity grow!

FAQs

Q: Can the Artist’s Color Wheel help with digital art?

A: Yes! Understanding traditional color relationships can improve your palettes in digital work too.

Q: Is the Pocket Wheel too small for detailed reference?

A: Not at all. It’s compact but still clear and easy to read, making it great for quick on-the-go use.

Q: Is the Classroom Wheel only for teaching?

A: No, it’s also great for large studios where multiple artists can reference it at once.
