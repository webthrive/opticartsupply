Winsor & Newton Artisan Paints – Oil Colors for Clean & Safe Studio Work

By Colin · 27 May 2025 · 4 min read

Shop Winsor & Newton Artisan Paints today – professional oil colors that clean up with water, not solvents. Smooth blending, and safer studio use.

Choosing the right oil paints can be a daunting task, especially for artists seeking the perfect balance between tradition and modern convenience. Winsor & Newton Artisan Water Mixable Oil Paints are a game-changer for artists who love oil painting but want to avoid the mess and toxicity of traditional solvents. This guide dives into why these paints are worth considering, their benefits, and tips to make the most of them.

What Makes Winsor & Newton Artisan Paints Unique?

Winsor & Newton is a globally recognized brand, renowned for its high-quality art supplies. Artisan Water Mixable Oil Paints stand out because they combine the richness of traditional oil paints with the convenience of water-based mediums.

Unlike conventional oil paints, Artisan paints are designed to mix and clean up with water—no need for harsh solvents like turpentine. This makes them an excellent choice for:

  • Artists with sensitivities to chemical fumes.
  • Beginners who want an easier cleanup process.
  • Eco-conscious individuals looking for a less toxic option.

Key Features and Benefits

1. Rich Pigmentation

Winsor & Newton Artisan Water Mixable Oil Paints provide vibrant, intense colors comparable to traditional oils. Winsor & Newton ensures consistent quality with highly pigmented formulas that retain their brilliance even after drying.

2. Versatility

These paints can be used with a range of water-mixable mediums like linseed oil, fast-drying mediums, and even impasto gels. Whether you’re creating thin glazes or thick, textured strokes, Artisan paints adapt to your style.

3. Ease of Use

  • Mixing: Combine colors effortlessly with water or Artisan mediums.
  • Cleaning: Brushes and tools clean up easily with soap and water—no special cleaning agents required.

4. Environmentally Friendly

Artisan paints reduce the environmental impact by eliminating the need for toxic solvents. This aligns with the growing trend of sustainable art practices.

Tips for Getting the Best Results

1. Prepare Your Surface

Artisan paints work beautifully on primed canvases and boards. Ensure your surface is properly prepared to avoid cracking or uneven textures.

2. Experiment with Mediums

Try water-mixable linseed oil to increase fluidity or a fast-drying medium to speed up the drying time.

3. Layering Techniques

Winsor & Newton Artisan Paints follow the traditional “fat over lean” rule for layering. Use thinner, water-diluted layers first, followed by thicker, medium-enriched layers.

4. Storage and Care

Store your paints in a cool, dry place and ensure tubes are tightly sealed to prevent drying out.

Fewquently Asked Questions

1. Can I mix Winsor & Newton Artisan paints with traditional oil paints?

While it’s possible to mix them, doing so will reduce their water-mixable properties, requiring the use of solvents for cleanup.

2. How long do these paints take to dry?

Drying times depend on the thickness of the application, but generally, Artisan paints dry slightly faster than traditional oil paints.

3. Are these paints suitable for beginners?

Yes! The ease of use, water cleanup, and vibrant colors make them an excellent choice for beginners.

4. Do I need special brushes for these paints?

No, standard brushes for oil paints work perfectly. Just clean them with water and soap after use.
