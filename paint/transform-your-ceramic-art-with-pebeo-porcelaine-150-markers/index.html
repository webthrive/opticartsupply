What Are Pebeo Porcelaine 150 Paint Markers?

The Paint Markers are specially designed for painting and decorating non-porous surfaces such as porcelain, glass, and ceramic. They provide excellent coverage, high durability, and a professional finish that makes your designs pop. Whether you're personalizing mugs, designing plates, or creating intricate glass art, these markers are the perfect tool for the job.

Key Features That Make the Porcelaine 150 Stand Out

Ease of Use:

  • These markers feature a precision tip, making them suitable for detailed work as well as bold strokes.
  • The paint flows smoothly, ensuring consistent application without streaks.

Vibrant Colors:

  • The Pebeo Porcelaine 150 is available in a wide range of shades, from bright hues to metallic tones, offering endless creative possibilities.

Durability:

  • Once heat set in a domestic oven at 150°C (300°F), the designs become permanent, dishwasher-safe, and resistant to fading or scratching.

Non-Toxic Formula:

  • The markers are water-based and non-toxic, making them safe for home use, even around kids (with supervision).

How to Use Pebeo Porcelaine 150 Paint Markers?

Prepare the Surface:

Clean your surface thoroughly with soap and water or rubbing alcohol to remove dirt and grease.

Shake and Test:

Shake the marker well to mix the paint. Test it on a piece of paper to ensure smooth paint flow.

Apply Your Design:

Draw or write directly on your chosen surface. The fine tip allows for intricate designs.

Heat Set for Permanence:

Allow your design to dry for at least 24 hours. Then, place the item in a cold oven, set to 150°C (300°F), and bake for 35 minutes. Let it cool inside the oven.

FAQs

1. Are Pebeo Porcelaine 150 Paint Markers safe for food-contact surfaces?

It’s recommended to avoid decorating areas that will come in direct contact with food, such as the inside of plates or cups. Focus on external surfaces for safety.

2. Can these markers be used on other surfaces besides porcelain and glass?

Yes, they work well on other non-porous surfaces like ceramics and glazed tiles. However, they’re not ideal for porous materials like wood or fabric.

3. What should I do if the paint tip dries out?

Simply shake the marker and press the tip gently on a piece of paper until the paint flows smoothly again.

4. Can I fix mistakes while designing?

Yes, before heat-setting, you can remove mistakes with a damp cloth or cotton swab. Once baked, the design becomes permanent.
