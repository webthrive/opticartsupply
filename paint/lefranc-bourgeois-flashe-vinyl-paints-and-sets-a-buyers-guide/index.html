About Lefranc & Bourgeois: A Legacy of Excellence

Lefranc & Bourgeois is a name synonymous with quality and innovation in the art world. Founded in 1720, this French brand has over three centuries of expertise in producing art materials that inspire creativity. From beginners to seasoned professionals, artists trust Lefranc & Bourgeois for their commitment to craftsmanship and high standards. Their Flashe range continues this tradition, offering exceptional performance across various artistic disciplines.

What Are Flashe Vinyl Paints?

Flashe paints are a unique type of vinyl-based paint, known for their:

  • High Pigmentation: These paints deliver unparalleled color intensity and coverage.
  • Matte Finish: Flashe paints dry to a velvety matte surface, giving artworks a modern and professional look.
  • Versatility: Suitable for a variety of surfaces, including canvas, paper, wood, and even walls.
  • Durability: The paint film is flexible and resistant to cracking, ensuring your creations last over time.

Key Features That Set Lefranc & Bourgeois Flashe Vinyl Paints Apart

Exceptional Coverage

Flashe paints are highly concentrated, providing excellent coverage with minimal layers. This makes them ideal for large projects or detailed work where precision matters.

Fast Drying

Unlike traditional acrylics, these paints dry quickly, allowing artists to work efficiently without long waiting periods.

Mixability

These paints can be blended easily to create custom colors, expanding your creative possibilities.

Water-Resistant Finish

Once dry, the paint becomes water-resistant, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor applications.

Who Can Benefit from Flashe Paints?

  • Fine Artists: Perfect for creating bold, flat color fields and intricate details.
  • Illustrators: Ideal for achieving consistent textures and smooth finishes.
  • Muralists: The water-resistant properties make these paints a go-to choice for outdoor projects.
  • Crafters: Use the paints to add a unique touch to handmade items, from furniture to decorative objects.

How to Use Flashe Paints?

Surface Preparation

Ensure your surface is clean and primed for better adhesion.

Application Techniques

Use brushes, palette knives, or even sponges for different textures and effects.
