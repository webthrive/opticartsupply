What Are Amaco Lead-Free Velvet Underglazes?

Amaco Velvet Underglazes are ceramic underglazes renowned for their vibrant colors, smooth application, and versatility. The "velvet" in the name refers to their soft, matte finish when left unglazed. Unlike traditional glazes, underglazes act more like paints, offering a broader spectrum of artistic possibilities.

One standout feature of Amaco’s velvet line is its lead-free formula, making it a safer option for artists who prioritize non-toxic materials. This safety feature also makes it ideal for schools, studios, and home use.

Why Choose Amaco Velvet Underglazes?

Here are some compelling reasons why artists swear by these underglazes:

Vibrant Color Range

With over 50 available hues, from bold primary tones to subtle pastels, these underglazes cater to any artistic vision.

Consistency Across Temperatures

Whether you’re working at low fire (cone 05) or high fire (cone 10) temperatures, these underglazes maintain their integrity, offering reliable results.

Versatile Applications

Amaco Velvet Underglazes can be applied on greenware, bisque ware, or even leather-hard clay. They work well as a base color or as fine details painted over a glaze.

Mixable Shades

Want to experiment with colors? These underglazes are mixable, enabling you to create unique shades and gradients.

Food-Safe

If fired and glazed properly, they’re food-safe, opening possibilities for functional ceramics like plates, mugs, and bowls.

How to Use Amaco Velvet Underglazes?

Using these underglazes is straightforward, even for beginners. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Preparation

Clean your clay surface to remove dust or oils. This ensures better adherence.

Application

Use a soft brush to apply thin, even coats. Three coats are recommended for the best color payoff.

Layering Techniques

Experiment with blending or layering different colors for depth and texture.

Firing

Allow the underglaze to dry completely before firing. You can fire it before glazing or apply a clear glaze over the underglaze for a glossy finish.

Test

Always test a small piece first to understand how the colors will react to your specific firing conditions.

Popular Colors and Their Uses

Some colors from the Amaco Velvet line stand out for their unique effects and applications:

  • Jet Black: Perfect for fine details and bold contrasts.
  • Bright Red: A striking option for accents and highlights.
  • Aqua: Ideal for soothing, natural designs.
  • White: Excellent for mixing or as a base for layering.

Each color has its personality and finish, so exploring them can be an adventure in itself!

Tips for Maximizing Results

  • Dilution: Mix with water to create a watercolor effect or softer tones.
  • Layering: Apply multiple colors for intricate designs or fade effects.
  • Experimentation: Combine underglazes with different firing techniques, like sgraffito or mishima, for unique textures.
